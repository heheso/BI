============================================================ Part A: Data Visualization using Python ===========================================================

This script uses the pandas library to crunch the numbers (Aggregation) and matplotlib to draw the charts. It is looking for a file named Coffee_sales.csv.

import pandas as pd
import matplotlib.pyplot as plt

# Load the coffee sales data
df = pd.read_csv('Coffe_sales.csv')

# --- 1. Visualization: Total Revenue by Coffee Type ---
# Group by coffee name and sum the money, then sort by revenue (descending)
coffee_revenue = df.groupby('coffee_name')['money'].sum().sort_values(ascending=False).reset_index()

plt.bar(coffee_revenue['coffee_name'], coffee_revenue['money'], color='skyblue')
plt.title('Total Revenue by Coffee Type')
plt.xlabel('Coffee Name')
plt.ylabel('Total Revenue ($)')
plt.xticks(rotation=45, ha='right')
plt.tight_layout()
plt.savefig('coffee_revenue.png')
plt.close()

# --- 2. Visualization: Total Revenue by Day of Week ---
# Group by Weekday and Weekdaysort to ensure bars are in chronological order
weekday_revenue = df.groupby(['Weekday', 'Weekdaysort'])['money'].sum().reset_index().sort_values('Weekdaysort')

plt.bar(weekday_revenue['Weekday'], weekday_revenue['money'], color='orange')
plt.title('Total Revenue by Day of Week')
plt.xlabel('Day of Week')
plt.ylabel('Total Revenue ($)')
plt.tight_layout()
plt.savefig('weekday_revenue.png')
plt.close()

print("Visualizations saved as 'coffee_revenue.png' and 'weekday_revenue.png'.")



=========================================================Part B: Data Visualization using Power BI================================================================


### Step 1: Extract (Get the Data)

Instead of connecting to the OData web feed like the manual did, we will load your CSV file.

1. Open Power BI Desktop.
2. On the Home ribbon, click **Get Data** > **Text/CSV**.
3. Find and select your `Coffe_sales.csv` file, then click **Open**.
4. A preview window will pop up. Don't click "Load" just yet—click **Transform Data** to open the Power Query Editor.


### Step 2: Transform (Clean the Data)

In the Power Query Editor, we need to make sure Power BI understands what type of data is in each column.

1. Check the icon next to the **`money`** column header. It should be a decimal number (usually represented by `1.2`). If it's `ABC` (Text), click the `ABC` icon and change it to **Decimal Number** or **Fixed Decimal Number** (Currency).
2. Check **`Weekdaysort`**. It should be a **Whole Number** (`123`).
3. If everything looks good, click **Close & Apply** in the top left corner of the Home ribbon.


### Step 3: The "Magic Trick" (Data Modeling)

1. Go to the **Data View** (the table icon on the far left edge of the screen).
2. Click on the **`Weekday`** column header so it is highlighted.
3. On the top ribbon, look for **Sort by Column** and click it.
4. Select **`Weekdaysort`** from the dropdown list. Now, Power BI knows that Monday (1) comes before Tuesday (2)!


### Step 4: Visualize (Building the Dashboard)


**Chart 1: Total Revenue by Coffee Type**

1. In the **Visualizations** pane on the right, click the **Clustered Column Chart** icon (it looks like vertical bars). A blank box will appear on your canvas.
2. From the **Fields** pane (far right), drag **`coffee_name`** into the **X-axis** box.
3. Drag **`money`** into the **Y-axis** box.
4. *Boom!* Power BI automatically sums up the money for each coffee type. You can click the "..." at the top right of the visual to "Sort Axis" by Money (Descending) to see your top sellers first.

**Chart 2: Total Revenue by Day of Week**

1. Click anywhere on the blank white canvas so you don't overwrite your first chart.
2. Click the **Clustered Column Chart** icon again to make a second blank box.
3. Drag **`Weekday`** to the **X-axis**.
4. Drag **`money`** to the **Y-axis**.
5. Because of our magic trick in Step 3, your bars will perfectly flow from Monday to Sunday!
